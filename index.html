All my life I have wanted to be a teacher, always wanting to help those around me. When I entered high school though, I got involved with the club on campus for making connections with our fellow classmates with disabilities. We would put on Special Olympics events, have them exposed to social situations, and have other fun activities like dances or enjoying free time during class. My favorite part about it though, was that when I would be walking around campus, some of my peers with limited vocabulary would say hi to me. That made me feel like I had a big impact on their lives and that they felt comfortable enough to speak with me. I enjoyed it so much, and it made me specifically want to go into teaching Special Education.

I decided on becoming a Liberal Studies major so that I can have a broad teaching education. Once I start working as a teacher after graduation, I would like to get my masters degree in Mod-Severe Special Education, so that I can expand my knowledge even more on the subject.
